The Ultimate IML Packaging Guide

Exploring how In Mould Labeling transcends traditional packaging to become an essential brand asset for the modern tactile world.

In the competitive landscape of food, dairy, and FMCG sectors, the package is the first handshake between your brand and the consumer. In Mould Labeling (IML) is not just a technique; it is a fusion of durability and artistic expression. By integrating the label directly into the container walls during the manufacturing process, we eliminate the fragility of surface-applied stickers.

The future of packaging belongs to those who prioritize structural integrity without sacrificing visual fidelity. IML creates a seamless, mono-material finish that is both 100% recyclable and impervious to moisture, temperature fluctuations, and abrasion.

As consumer demand shifts toward sustainable yet premium experiences, Taigus provides the technical bridge to elevate your brand's presence on the shelf and in the home.

The Advantages

Unmatched Durability

Unlike adhesive labels, IML labels are resistant to moisture, high-humidity, and freezing temperatures. They cannot be peeled, scratched, or damaged during transit, ensuring brand integrity from factory to shelf.

Aesthetic Excellence

High-resolution graphics wrap perfectly around complex shapes. From photographic realism to metallic finishes, IML offers a premium look that commands attention in a crowded retail aisle.

Tamper Evidence

Because the label is an integral part of the container, any attempt to alter the branding or surface is immediately visible, providing an extra layer of consumer safety and trust.

Infinite Recyclability

Since the container and label are made of the same material (PP), the entire package can be recycled as a single unit without the need for label removal, supporting a circular economy.
