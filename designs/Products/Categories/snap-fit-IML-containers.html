The Science of the Snap.

Unlike traditional press-fit lids, Taigus Snap Fit containers utilize a specialized lip/flange mechanism. When pressed, the lid undergoes a controlled deformation before locking into the container rim with an audible click.

This mechanical lock is perfectly integrated with our In-Mold Labeling (IML) process. The label becomes an inseparable part of the container wall, ensuring a seamless, premium finish that won't peel or bubble, even in deep-freeze environments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Snap Fit containers truly 100% leak-proof?

expand_more

Yes. The dual-flange mechanical seal is designed to withstand 5psi of pressure, ensuring liquid products like yogurt or sauces remain contained even during turbulent transit.

What is the Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ)?

expand_more

Standard MOQ starts at 25,000 units for standard colors and 50,000 units for custom IML prints, though we can accommodate smaller test runs for pilot launches.

Can these withstand microwave heating?

expand_more

Our PP material is microwave-safe for reheating purposes. However, they are not intended for cooking at high temperatures for extended periods.

How does IML compare to traditional labeling?

expand_more

IML is fused with the plastic during molding. It is tamper-proof, humidity-resistant, and offers superior image resolution compared to adhesive labels or direct printing.
