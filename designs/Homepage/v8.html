IML Manufacturing Process

From Artwork to Shelf Ready Container

In Mould Labelling combines container moulding and decoration in one precise cycle. A pre printed PP label is placed inside the mould, molten food grade PP is injected, and the label fuses with the container wall to create a seamless, durable branded pack.

IML vs Normal Containers

Why IML Outperforms Traditional Labelling

For ice cream, dairy, ghee, spreads, protein powders and FMCG products, packaging must survive filling, stacking, condensation, cold storage, transport and retail handling. IML gives brands a stronger, cleaner and more premium alternative to post applied sticker or sleeve labels.

Technical Metric IML Containers Traditional Sticker / Sleeve Labels
Label Durability Bond strength during handling Label is fused with the container wall, giving a permanent integrated finish. Post applied labels can peel, tear, wrinkle or lift at the edges during use.
Water & Chill Resistance Useful for freezer and refrigerated products Performs well against humidity, condensation and temperature changes. Adhesives and paper based labels may weaken in wet or high humidity conditions.
Graphic Quality Retail shelf impact High resolution, photo quality decoration with seamless container coverage. Finish depends on label surface, adhesive quality and application accuracy.
Production Efficiency Manufacturing flow Container forming and decoration happen in a single moulding cycle. Requires secondary labelling, sleeving or sticker application after moulding.
Food Packaging Hygiene Clean surface and no label feel No external glue layer, no exposed label edge, and a smooth integrated surface. Visible label edges and adhesive layers can reduce the premium clean pack look.
Brand Premium Consumer perception Seamless, high end finish that makes packaging look printed into the container. Standard consumer finish with visible label boundaries.
Sustainability Path Material compatibility PP label with PP container supports a mono material packaging approach. Mixed label materials and adhesives may need extra consideration during recycling.
