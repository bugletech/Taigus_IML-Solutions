### **2\. Category Overview**

**H2:** What Makes Snap Fit IML Containers the Smart Choice?

The snap fit mechanism works through a precisely engineered lip and flange system. When the lid is pressed down, it locks with an audible click, creating a secure, tamper-evident seal. This design is especially valued in the food and dairy industry for preventing leaks, maintaining freshness, and giving consumers confidence.

At Taigus, we integrate this proven closure with advanced In Mould Labelling (IML**)**. The label becomes part of the container wall during moulding resulting in a seamless, high-resolution finish that resists moisture, scratches, and condensation perfect for freezer and refrigerated display.

### **3\. Market Insights (New Section – Backed by Research)**

**H2:** Why Snap Fit IML Containers Are Growing Fast in India

* India’s ice cream market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 13 to 16% through 2033, driven by rising disposable incomes, urbanization, and premiumization.  
* Shift to Rigid Structures: Rigid plastic containers are currently expanding at a 7.61% CAGR in India, outpacing general flexible packaging in the dairy sector. # **Introduction: Why IML Packaging is the Future**

This packaging overview defines In Mould Labelling (IML) technology and explains why it has become the gold standard for plastic packaging insights in sectors like food, dairy, and FMCG. IML is not just a labeling process; it is a fundamental shift in IML packaging basics that delivers superior durability, quality, and brand integration.

In this IML packaging guide, you will learn the core principles of IML, its technical advantages over traditional labeling methods, and how applying these packaging best practices can directly benefit your product's shelf presence and integrity.

# **The Core Mechanics of IML Technology**

In Mould Labeling is a process where a pre printed label is inserted into the mold cavity before the molten plastic is injected. When the plastic is injected, it fuses with the label, making the label an integral, permanent part of the container wall.

## **The IML Process: A Step by Step Breakdown**

1. Label Preparation: A static charge or vacuum holds the pre printed plastic label inside the open mold.  
2. Injection: Molten plastic resin is injected into the mold.  
3. Fusion: The hot resin melts the back of the label, fusing it seamlessly with the container wall.  
4. Ejection: The finished, labeled container is ejected, ready for immediate use.

This precise process ensures perfect alignment and full coverage graphics, essential for maintaining brand consistency during mass production.

# **Key IML Packaging Benefits for Your Business**

The seamless integration of the label provides practical advantages that solve common packaging challenges:

* Superior Durability: Since the label is part of the plastic, it is highly resistant to scratching, scuffing, moisture, and temperature changes ideal for frozen foods or damp environments.  
* Aesthetic Appeal: IML allows for photographic quality, high resolution graphics and offers diverse finishes (glossy, matte, metallic), significantly boosting shelf appeal.  
* Tamper Evidence: For containers with snap fit IML lids, the inherent quality of the fused label reinforces product integrity.  
* Recyclability: The container and the label are often made from the same material (PP/PE), making the final product 100% recyclable.
